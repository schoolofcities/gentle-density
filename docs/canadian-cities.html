Provincial Policy

British Columbia

British Columbia (BC) is Canada’s westernmost province, reaching 1,200 km north to south and 1,050km from east to west. The province is home to 5.65 million people and of that, 5.4% are cultural workers making BC home to the largest population of cultural workers in Canada.

BC is currently suffering from a housing crisis with the Vancouver Sun reporting that 23% of renters spend more than 30% on housing and 42% spend more than 50% of their net income on housing. To combat the lack of affordable housing options, the province has created a new Policy Manual & Site Standards for Small-Scale, Multi-Unit Housing (SSMUH). Under the policy, SSMUH includes secondary suites, ADU’s, triplexes, townhomes, and house-plexes. Through the development of these housing types, the province hopes to provide more housing options for low to middle-income families, for aging parents, adult children or other family members, helping to pay a mortgage or investing in existing property and contributing to more long-term rental housing in communities.

Secondary Suites under BC legislation is defined as an independent living unit with its own kitchen, sleeping area, and washroom facilities contained within another dwelling. An ADU is defined as a self-contained dwelling unit, such as a garden suite, laneway home, carriage house or garage suite, located on the same legal lot as another dwelling.

All policies and procedures for the construction of a secondary suite or ADU can be found in the Home Suite Home: Guidelines on How to Add and Manage a Secondary Suite or Accessory Dwelling Unit in British Columbia.

In the report, funding for secondary suites and ADU’s is discussed as attainable through the Secondary Suite Incentive Program (SSIP). The SSIP provides financial assistance to homeowners who wish to build long-term affordable rental units. Homeowners can receive up to 50% on the cost of renovations, up to a maximum of $40,000. Money is given through a rebate in the form of loan does not to be repaid if the homeowner follows the terms of the program.
